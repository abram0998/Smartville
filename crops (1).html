Major Crops in Uganda: Prices, Seasons & Growing Areas

Crop Typical Price (UGX / kg) Growing Seasons Main Growing Regions
Maize ~ 1,300 UGX/kg (farmgate) 1st season (Mar–Aug), 2nd season (Aug–Dec) Nationwide, especially in central & eastern regions
Beans (Dry: Nambaale, Masavu, Yellow) 3,500–4,800 UGX/kg (farmgate) 1st & 2nd seasons (intercropped with maize often) Western & Central Uganda
Cassava (Chips) 450–500 UGX/kg 2 seasons — similar planting calendar as other staples Nationwide; especially in eastern Uganda
Banana (Matooke) ~ 1,200 UGX/kg (during festive season) Perennial (plantains / matooke grown year-round; food banana) Western and Central Uganda
Sorghum 1,500–1,800 UGX/kg 1st & 2nd season Some production in northern and northeastern Uganda
Millet 2,900–3,500 UGX/kg 1st & 2nd seasons Scattered in drier or semi-arid zones
Rice (Paddy) 2,800–4,500 UGX/kg (super / Pakistan rice) 1st & 2nd season Irrigated areas, lowland rice zones
Groundnuts 5,000–5,500 UGX/kg (Red) 1st & 2nd season Various regions — smallholder production widespread
Sim Sim (Sesame) 4,800–5,300 UGX/kg 1st & 2nd season Some production zones; oilseed farmers
Sweet Potatoes ~100,000–150,000 UGX per bag 1st & 2nd season Many regions; root crop farmers
Irish Potatoes 100,000–250,000 UGX per bag 1st & 2nd season Highland / cool zones (e.g. western Uganda)
Coffee (Robusta & Arabica) ~14,000 UGX/kg (average) Perennial / permanent crop Central, East, West highlands
Vanilla 5,000–6,000 UGX/kg Perennial (vanilla vines) Emerging in different regions

Notes / Caveats:

  • Prices are approximate and vary by region, market, season, and quality.
  • Seasons follow Uganda’s long rainy (Mar–Aug) and short rainy (Aug–Dec) cycles.
  • Growing areas are generalized.
  • Some crops (like vanilla) are emerging cash crops.
